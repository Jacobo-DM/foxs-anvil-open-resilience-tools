Open Resilience Tools

The Fox’s Anvil is a commons-based workshop for tools that meet the world at the scale of land, water, emergency response, and community repair.

These are not products, wearables, or toys. They are open designs for real work: restoration, flood and fire response, agroecology, local memory, mutual aid, and community resilience.

All designs shared here are meant to be built, repaired, adapted, and improved by farmers, welders, mechanics, librarians, students, neighbors, volunteers, and communities willing to take responsibility for their use.

Tools in this Forge

  • The Spoovel · V1 A low-tech, field-repairable tool for moving, filling, shaping, or placing material in restoration and resilience work.
  • Signal Finder + CodeTape · v0.1 A commons seed for emergency signal-finding, paper-first communication, local stewardship, and offline community memory.
  • Additional tools More tools will be added as they become clear enough to share, test, and repair in public.

Relation to MythWear

MythWear Commons focuses on body-scale tools, garments, and movement companions. The Fox’s Anvil focuses on land-scale, field-scale, and infrastructure-scale tools.

They are siblings: one close to the skin, one close to the ground.
