# 🛠️ Fox’s Anvil — Open Resilience Tools

**Low-tech tools for high-stakes times.
Buildable by communities. Repairable by hand. Open to everyone.**

Fox’s Anvil is a commons-based workshop for tools that meet the world at the
scale of land, water, emergency response, restoration, civic clarity,
nourishment, and community repair. These are not products or solutions — they
are open designs and invitations to skill-sharing, autonomy, and collective repair.

**Working principle:** build small, test honestly, repair locally, share what
fails as well as what works.

---

## 🔥 What Lives Here

Each of these has a page in the repository. Most are early — seeds, `v0`
drafts, and concepts meant to be built, tested, and improved in public, not
finished products.

- **Signal Finder + CodeTape** — emergency signal-finding, paper-first
  communication, local stewardship, and offline community memory
- **Rescue Thread** — establishing contact with trapped people when direct
  access is unsafe or impossible (Signal Finder locates; Rescue Thread reaches)
- **Garden Raft** — a low-tech mediating platform for water-edge work: pond
  and wetland tending, flooded gardens, field study, habitat repair
- **Ocean Listening Network** — a story-rooted concept for listening to marine
  life and witnessing harm without enclosing the knowledge in a distant platform
- **Civic Care Commons** — child care treated as distributed civic
  infrastructure: community-rooted, dignity-preserving, access-aware
- **Gofu Commons Kitchen** — a humble food-resilience nest: commons food,
  shared and camp kitchens, community meals, and food sovereignty
- **Civic Witness Charter** — a public clarity, evidence-preservation, and
  boundary tool for moments when noise overwhelms evidence
- **The Spoovel** — a field-repairable sack-filling and material-handling tool path

**Emerging / not yet published here:** Commons Conveyer (hand-driven conveyor
for soil, compost, grain), jute-based field kits, and village-scale repairs &
fixtures. These are intended directions, not yet built pages — they will be
added as they become clear enough to share, test, and steward responsibly.

What guides everything here:

- Adaptable to place, climate, and culture
- Inexpensive and buildable with locally available materials
- Repairable by hand
- Honest about what is tested and what is still a seed

## 🦊 Why “Fox’s Anvil”?

The fox is a symbol of subtlety, adaptability, play, and intelligence.

The anvil is grounding, practical, world-facing, and steady.

Together they form a philosophy:
**regenerative tools made with humility, creativity, and community sense.**
