
# 🛠️ Fox’s Anvil — Open Resilience Tools  
**Low-tech tools for high-stakes times.  
Buildable by communities. Repairable by hand. Open to everyone.**

Fox’s Anvil gathers simple, durable designs meant to strengthen local resilience: water, food, restoration, soil, movement, craft, and care. These tools are not solutions — they are invitations to skill-sharing, autonomy, and collective repair.

---

## 🔥 What Lives Here
- **Spoovel / Spuval** — sack-filling and binding mechanism  
- **Signal Finder + Codetape** — emergency signal mapping with local stewardship and paper + wireless layers  
- **Commons Conveyer** — low-tech, hand-driven conveyor for soil, compost, grain  
- **Jute-based field kits**  
- **Village-scale repairs & fixtures**  
- **Printable schematics and build notes**  

Everything here is:
- Field-tested  
- Adaptable  
- Inexpensive  
- Buildable with locally available materials  

---

## 🧩 Structure
- `/signal-finder` — Signal Finder + Codetape seed document  
- `/spoovel` — Spoovel overview and schematic  
- `/schematics` — PDFs, SVGs, and illustrations  
- `/prototypes` — photos, measurements, stresses, field notes  
- `/materials` — what to source, alternatives, trade-offs  
- `/instructions` — step-by-step builds  
- `/research` — inspirations, precedents, mechanical notes

## 🦊 Why “Fox’s Anvil”?
The fox is a symbol of:
- subtlety  
- adaptability  
- play  
- intelligence  

The anvil is:
- grounding  
- practical  
- world-facing  
- steady  

Together they form a philosophy:  
**regen tools made with humility, creativity, and community sense.**
