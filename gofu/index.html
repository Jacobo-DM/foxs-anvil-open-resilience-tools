Gofu — Commons Kitchen Offering

Gofu is a small food commons seed: a simple, adaptable, plant-based kitchen offering related to chickpea tofu and sesame-tofu traditions, intended for field kitchens, school kitchens, care spaces, camps, gardens, and community gatherings.

Root note: Food becomes infrastructure when it helps people stay, learn, gather, recover, and care for one another.

Why it belongs in Fox’s Anvil

Fox’s Anvil holds open resilience tools. Some tools are rafts, tethers, signal systems, or field schematics. Some are quieter: a recipe that can be taught, adapted, repaired, and carried into a kitchen where people need nourishment without spectacle.

Gofu belongs here as a commons food offering because it can support care work: child-care meals, field days, community gardens, emergency kitchens, after-school gatherings, and low-cost experiments in nourishment that do not require expensive equipment or proprietary ingredients.

What it is

Gofu is not a brand and not a product. It is a working name for a simple food pathway: a flexible, teachable preparation that can be tested in small kitchens, documented clearly, adapted locally, and shared under commons terms.

Start with one clean batch.
Feed real people.
Record what worked.
Record what failed.
Make the next batch easier to teach.

Design constraints

  • Use ordinary kitchen tools whenever possible.
  • Keep ingredients affordable, legible, and locally adaptable.
  • Document allergen risks clearly, especially sesame, soy, nuts, and gluten where relevant.
  • Make the recipe teachable by a community cook, student, elder, caregiver, or volunteer.
  • Preserve dignity: food offered through the commons should not feel like charity leftovers.
  • Share failures honestly so other kitchens do not have to repeat them.

Possible first test

A first public version could be a small kitchen field test: one base recipe, one flavor variation, one simple serving context, and one page of notes on cost, time, texture, acceptance, allergies, cleanup, and what should change next.

v0 question: Can a small kitchen make one batch of gofu that is affordable, nourishing, pleasant to eat, easy to explain, and easy to repeat?

Commons relation

Gofu can connect directly to Civic Care Commons, Garden Raft gatherings, school field days, neighborhood kitchens, and resilience events. It is a food seed, but it is also a coordination seed: a way for kitchens, caregivers, gardeners, teachers, and neighbors to meet around something practical.

The offering is small on purpose.

If it works, it can travel.

Shared in the spirit of the Commons. Build with care. Test honestly. Use with responsibility.
