Spoovel · Schematic (V1)

This page is the working HTML schematic for the Spoovel mechanism. It is meant to stay simple, readable, and easy to update. Later, this can be printed to PDF if needed, but for now this HTML page is the source of truth.

Purpose

  • Low-tech, field-repairable sack-filling tool.
  • Supports flood response, erosion control, and restoration work.
  • Reduces strain on workers compared to bending and scooping by hand.

Main Parts

  • Frame with front hooks to hold the sack at working height.
  • Chute or funnel that guides soil or material into the sack.
  • Cam / lever or pedal that gives a repeatable “cycle” for shake + close.
  • Simple closure area where the sack is twisted and tied, then released.

One Work Cycle (Example)

  1. Hang empty sack on the front hooks.
  2. Shovel material into the chute until sack is near the fill mark.
  3. Use the pedal/lever once to shake/settle the load.
  4. Gather the top of the sack, twist or fold to close.
  5. Tie or strap the closure, then release the sack to a pallet or slide.
  6. Hang the next empty sack and repeat.

This schematic is intentionally brief. More detailed drawings, measurements, and variations can be added here over time as the design is tested in the field.
