Signal Finder + Codetape

A community-held emergency signal awareness system designed to help people locate surviving communication, need, and refuge signals after disaster — without turning crisis into surveillance infrastructure.

  • Seed document
  • Version 0.1
  • Three layers
  • Local steward required
  • CC BY-SA 4.0

Purpose

Find the signal. Protect the people. Signal Finder is meant for crisis conditions where communication has fractured and local responders need to understand what signals remain: wireless traces, visible tags, handwritten messages, safe-house markers, dead-zone corridors, and resource points.

The system is not a cloud platform. It is a handoff discipline: detection becomes stewardship, and the map belongs first to the affected community.

Process Rhythm

01 · Find

Sense wireless and visible signals across the affected area.

02 · Read

Distinguish infrastructure, beacon, tag, and need. Not content. Not identity.

03 · Hand Off

Return the map to the local steward. Not the cloud. Not a remote platform.

04 · Repair

Use the map to reconnect people, resources, and care.

Local Steward Map

Data belongs first to the affected community. Data flows to a trusted local steward — a clinic, library, school, fire station, mutual-aid hub, or neighborhood council chosen by the people affected. Not assigned by outside responders or platforms.

  • Signal strength logs
  • Paper tag sightings
  • Emergency beacon locations
  • Dead-zone corridors
  • Resource points
  • Summary maps when appropriate
  • Deletion after emergency window
  • No raw export without steward consent

The map belongs to the people in the map.

Codetape

Codetape is the trust layer. Signal data stays close to the people affected. The system may find signals, but authority over the map is handed to a trusted local steward — not to a cloud platform, vendor, or outside agency by default.

Codetape can include offline storage, printed handoff logs, local encryption keys, deletion protocols, paper records, and community-held access rules.

The signal can travel. The authority to hold the map does not.

Paper Layer

Some signs everyone knows. Some signs only this place knows. The paper tag system carries two registers: universal signs any person can read on arrival, and local signs created by the community before disaster season.

The paper layer is not a fallback for when wireless fails. It is a first-class protocol. It predates the internet, survives all power outages, and requires no infrastructure to receive.

Water · medical · food · safe place · danger · local symbols

Responsible Use Covenant

This system does not:

  • Conduct surveillance
  • Upload to cloud by default
  • Identify individuals unless rescue requires it
  • Retain data post-crisis without consent
  • Require an account, app, login, or platform to receive help
  • Operate without a named local steward
  • Export raw data without steward consent
  • Operate outside emergency context without steward approval

Pilot Zones

  • Earthquake neighborhood grid
  • Wildfire evacuation edge
  • Flooded street / rural road
  • Blackout corridor
  • Mutual-aid shelter network
  • Island / ferry-dependent community

Prior Relatives

This seed has relatives in commercial search-and-rescue signal drones, disaster mesh networking, open-source UAV ecosystems, and Wi-Fi/Bluetooth emergency-response research.

Its commons contribution is the ethical frame: local stewardship, paper-layer dignity, privacy by design, and community-held maps.
