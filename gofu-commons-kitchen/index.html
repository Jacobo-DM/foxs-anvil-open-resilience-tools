Gofu Commons Kitchen

A small food-resilience offering rooted in simple nourishment, local adaptation, shared kitchens, and commons care. Gofu begins as a humble bean-based preparation, but opens toward field kitchens, child care, community meals, and dignified food support.

Root note: Food becomes infrastructure when it helps people stay, learn, gather, recover, and care for one another.

Why it belongs in Fox’s Anvil

Resilience is not only rescue, sensors, rafts, and signal systems. It is also food, dignity, and care. Gofu is the soft, nourishing tool in this forge: a teachable, repairable food practice that supports community kitchens, camp kitchens, the Civic Care Commons, and any gathering where nourishment is part of staying resilient together.

What lives here, and what lives elsewhere

This page is a nest, not the whole tree. The Fox’s Anvil holds a short landing that places Gofu in the resilience ecosystem. Canonical-repository principle

The canonical home of Gofu Commons is its own repository. This Anvil page is a nest that points outward. Mirrors, excerpts, and local adaptations are welcome, but they should defer to the canonical Gofu Commons as the reference version, and link back to it.

Commons relation

Gofu connects directly to the Civic Care Commons, to garden gatherings, school field days, neighborhood kitchens, and resilience events. It is a food seed and a coordination seed at once: a practical thing for kitchens, caregivers, gardeners, teachers, and neighbors to meet around.
